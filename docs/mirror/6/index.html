When AI 'recommends' something, do you treat it as a suggestion or a default?

Choice architecture is a well-studied field. The options you see, the order they appear, the defaults that are pre-selectedâ€”all of these shape choices more than conscious deliberation. AI supercharges this: it learns which architectures work on you. Defaults are powerful. Most people accept defaults. AI recommendations are framed as 'suggestions,' but they function as defaults: they're the easiest path, and deviation requires effort. Over time, the path of least resistance becomes the only path you remember exists.

Your choices are still yours. But the menu was written for you, personally, by a system that knows what you'll order.
