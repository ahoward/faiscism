What you think you're doing / What the system is doing

When an AI tool gets better at predicting what you want, how do you feel?

When you use AI, you're also: generating training data, providing feedback on outputs, normalizing the tool's patterns, creating dependency, and accepting its constraints as defaults. The question isn't whether these are bad. It's whether you noticed them. An AI that predicts you well has modeled you well. That model exists on someone else's server, informs someone else's business decisions, and nudges you toward predictable patterns because predictable users are valuable users.

'Personalization' and 'control' are the same mechanism, described from different positions.
